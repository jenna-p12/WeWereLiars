Themes & Theory

Color and Symbolism

Blue/Grey: Represents Cadence's emotional state of confusion and sadness. The cloudy blue mirrors her cloudy mind after the accident, symbolizing her struggle with memory and truth.

White: Symbolizes the Sinclair family's image of perfection and their pristine private island. This white facade hides the darker secrets and represents the family's obsession with appearances over reality.

Bright Pastels: Used in flashbacks to represent the carefree, luxurious life the family presents. These vibrant colors create a stark contrast to the underlying emotional pain and tragedy.

Dark Tones: Represent the hidden truth and the darker reality beneath the beautiful surface of Sinclair Island.

E. Lockhart uses this color symbolism throughout the novel to reinforce themes of deception, privilege, and memory loss.
